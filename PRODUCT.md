# Product

## Register

product

## User

Someone with years of Google Timeline history who wants to revisit real trips without sorting a log of coordinates or manufacturing a travel journal.

## Purpose

There I Was imports a Google Timeline JSON export, finds journeys between returns home, names the places that mattered, and replays the recorded route. GPT-5.6 can turn a selected trip's compact evidence dossier into an editable story draft. The route is evidence; the person supplies the meaning.

## Core journey

1. Import Timeline JSON.
2. Choose a trip named for its primary city, state, or country.
3. Replay the complete home-to-home path.
4. Optionally draft and edit a story.

There is no sample mode, onboarding tour, account, dashboard, or feature pitch.

## Product principles

1. Less interface, more trip.
2. Use real data or show nothing.
3. Name places before asking a person to interpret them.
4. Recorded route geometry is solid; missing geometry is visibly different.
5. Distance supports the story. It is never the story.
6. GPT-5.6 organizes evidence and asks for what telemetry cannot know. It does not invent.
7. Editing and dismissal are ordinary, visible actions.

## Voice

Plain, brief, and specific. No slogans, forced dichotomies, travel-blog sentiment, AI theater, “magic,” or system-oriented microcopy.

## Anti-references

- SaaS landing pages with eyebrow copy, giant claims, feature grids, and fake social proof.
- Fake trips, stock imagery, or generated placeholders.
- Timeline-style dumps of every recorded point.
- Hero metrics and opaque finale overlays.
- Chat interfaces for structured story editing.
- Coordinates presented in place of human place names.

## Accessibility

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Controls have visible focus and 44px targets. Playback can pause and scrub. Reduced-motion preferences are honored. Route provenance is communicated by line treatment as well as color. Desktop and mobile retain the same core tasks.
