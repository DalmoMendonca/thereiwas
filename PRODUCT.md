# Product

## Register

product

## Users

People who have years of Google Timeline history and want to revisit the journeys they already lived. They arrive curious rather than analytical: often after a vacation, around an anniversary, or while looking back across a season of life. Their primary job is to turn location evidence into an accurate, editable memory without having to organize photos, remember every detail, or learn a mapping tool.

## Product Purpose

There I Was transforms a Google Timeline JSON export into detected trips, reconstructed routes, cinematic replays, and grounded narrative structure. It should make dormant personal data feel emotionally useful while keeping the human in authority: telemetry supplies evidence, GPT-5.6 organizes that evidence, and the user restores the meaning the data cannot know. Success is a complete, delightful journey from sample or import to a replayable, editable story in minutes, with the raw Timeline processed locally.

## Brand Personality

Reflective, cinematic, and trustworthy. The product should feel like an exceptional travel documentary editor that already knows the route but is humble about what it cannot know. Its voice is concise, specific, observant, and warm without becoming sentimental or writing travel-blog copy.

## Anti-references

- Google Timeline as a utilitarian log: rows of visits, weak playback, little emotional structure.
- Generic SaaS dashboards with interchangeable statistic cards, sidebars, and setup funnels.
- Neon AI gradients, glassmorphism, chat-first interfaces, and copy that treats model output as magic.
- Scrapbook or photo-album interfaces that imply memories require media.
- Surveillance dashboards, developer consoles, and maps that foreground raw coordinates over human place names.
- Overwrought travel-journal aesthetics: parchment, stamps, doodles, handwritten scripts, or nostalgic decoration that competes with the journey.

## Design Principles

1. **The route is the hero.** Make movement, arrival, and time legible before adding supporting interface.
2. **Evidence before embellishment.** Every factual story beat is grounded, every inference is labeled, and uncertainty remains visible.
3. **Delight lives in playback.** Reserve the most expressive motion for the cinematic journey; the surrounding product stays calm and fast.
4. **Human authority is a feature.** Editing, reflection, and restoration are first-class, not corrections hidden behind settings.
5. **Timeline-only must feel complete.** Never design around missing photos or make optional media look like the source of meaning.
6. **Privacy should be felt through behavior.** Process locally, minimize what leaves the browser, and explain external requests at the moment they matter.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. All text and controls must maintain compliant contrast, keyboard access, visible focus, semantic structure, and minimum touch targets. Playback must support pausing, scrubbing, keyboard controls, and a reduced-motion mode that replaces camera choreography with immediate or crossfaded state changes. Location, mode, confidence, and certainty may never rely on color alone. Time and dates should be presented in local, understandable formats, and the interface must remain usable from small mobile screens through wide desktop displays.
