There I Was turns a Google Maps Timeline export into trips I can replay. This sample uses a pared-down excerpt of my own Timeline: my latest trips to California, New York, and Italy. Nothing in it was made up.

I will open California. The trip starts at home, follows the roads to the West Coast, and closes back at home. The gray line keeps the full route in view. When I press Play, the orange marker moves along it and the green trail shows where I have been.

I can add geotagged photos from the trip. Their date and location are read in the browser. Selecting a photo moves the replay to that moment and centers its position on the map.

Create story sends a small, coordinate-free dossier to GPT-5.6. It contains named places, dates, travel modes, and summarized movement. The model organizes the evidence into editable chapters and asks questions only I can answer. It does not receive my Timeline file, home location, path coordinates, or photos.

Google collected the record. There I Was gives me a way to relive it.
